2019 here we go!

Jan 15, 2019 • Dani

2018 has been an amazing year. Full of trips, adventures, new emotions and surprising experiences. I’ve met great new people and other has left. Personally, I’ve lived a lot of new experiences with Waynabox and I’ve visited places where I’d stay forever. It would take a full new post if I wanted to review my past 2018 actually, but I write down this for another reason. 2019 is already here and that’s a fact. I’m not a person who writes down his new year resolutions but this year it’s going to be different and I’m gonna try it (well, we are already at mid January). So here is my 2019 resolutions:

Write more post about UX research

Every time I like more and more to analyze and explore the field of UX. How the digital user (or not) interacts with the technology and the world around us. I think it’s a super important aspect in our lives and many times we don’t even stop to think about it. Actually, the field of UX is a very unscientific field, where, at best, A/B testing studies are done to assess whether a proposed solution is better than another to solve a problem based on a behavior made by a number of people but I think there is still much to improve and demonstrate. That’s why this year I’d like to improve Waynabox UX, analyze the changes and publish them here to bring the community a little more knowledge.

Travel more

I don’t think it makes this point. Lately, I have traveled a lot in Europe and this year I would like to discover cities and countries outside the continent to see clearer contrasts of culture, gastronomy and traditions. If all goes well, I will achieve it next month traveling to the U.S. and Mexico to work from there (here you have more info of what I’m talking about)

Build something for travelers

With each technology that appears, the new flight search engine that is born and new travel APIs a set of tools are generated being able to create new products for people who like to travel in a different way. That’s why this year I want to take advantage of those resources to build something that helps people who spend their lives traveling more.

Get in shape

Since some year ago I’ve been thinking about getting in shape (in the end, my daily activity until today is reduced to going from home to work, sitting for 8 hours and going from work to home) and I have always postponed (trying to justify it with some absurd excuse). Last November I started to run (I even did 60km a week) and I started to lose weight until a knee injury interrupted my workouts. Now that I’m recovered I’m ready to start running again.

That’s all! I think are all reasonable and hopefully, I’ll achieve all of them. I’ll be posting my progress around here, so stay tuned.
